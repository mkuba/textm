When interest rates rise
Janet Yellen makes the case for an interest rate rise in December
All eyes are now on the Federal Reserve's next meeting, on December 17, after Janet Yellen, the Fed's chair, gave a speech on Wednesday that clearly laid out the case for why now is the time for higher rates. The US economy has "recovered substantially" and is set for further growth and firmer inflation, she said.
This would be the first rise in official US interest rates since 2006.
When will rates rise?
There are different ways to guess. One of these is by look at the Fed Fund futures markets, where investors essentially bet on what level the interest rate will be in upcoming months
What does the market "think"?
Probability of a range of interest rate outcomes based on Fed Fund future contract prices
